First Meeting Etiquette

In France, first dates are usually chosen in public places such as cafes, restaurants, or parks. The French value first impressions, so dressing appropriately is very important. Men usually wear suits or at least neat casual clothes, while women dress carefully. When meeting for the first time, it is usual to shake hands to say hello, not to hug or kiss on the cheek, unless you have already been introduced by friends.

In conversation, the French like to discuss topics such as art, literature, philosophy, and food, avoiding sensitive topics such as politics and money. Maintain eye contact to show interest and respect for the other person. The French appreciate people who are confident but not arrogant, so maintaining moderate confidence and a sense of humor will be very popular.

Gift Selection

In France, expensive gifts are usually not necessary for the first date. A bouquet of flowers, a box of chocolates, or a bottle of good wine are all good choices. If you've dated several times, you can consider giving more meaningful gifts, such as a book, concert tickets, or a small accessory with special meaning.

The French pay attention to gift wrapping and presentation, so even small gifts should be carefully wrapped with a handwritten card. Avoid giving overly personal gifts like perfume or clothing unless you already know the person's preferences very well.

Understanding French Emotional Expression

The French are usually direct and passionate, but they also have their own way of expressing emotions. They like to use sweet words and romantic gestures to express love, such as giving flowers, writing love letters, or arranging surprise dates. The French also value body language very much, such as holding hands, hugging, and kissing on the cheek, which are all ways to express intimacy and love.

However, the French also value personal space and independence, so don't be too eager or clingy. Give each other enough space and time to develop feelings, and respect each other's feelings and decisions.
